Puranpoli Recipe

About Puranpoli

Puranpoli is a traditional Indian sweet flatbread made with a filling of chana dal (yellow split peas), jaggery, and cardamom, encased in a soft dough. It is usually served during festivals and special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup chana dal (yellow split peas)
  • 1 cup jaggery, grated
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  • 2 cups whole wheat flour
  • 1 tbsp oil or ghee (clarified butter)
  • Water as needed
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Boil the chana dal in water until soft and cooked through. Drain excess water.
  2. In a pan, heat the jaggery and cardamom powder until the jaggery melts and forms a smooth syrup.
  3. Add the cooked chana dal to the jaggery syrup and mix well. Cook until the mixture thickens and becomes dry. Set aside to cool.
  4. In a separate bowl, mix the whole wheat flour, turmeric powder, and a pinch of salt. Add water gradually to knead the dough. It should be soft but not sticky. Let the dough rest for 15-20 minutes.
  5. Divide the dough into small balls. Roll each ball into a small disc and place a spoonful of the cooled puran (filling) in the center. Carefully fold the dough around the filling and seal it. Roll it out again to form a flatbread.
  6. Heat a tawa or griddle and cook the rolled-out puranpoli on both sides, applying oil or ghee as needed.
  7. Serve hot with a dollop of ghee on top!

Enjoy your delicious Puranpoli! Made with love and tradition.
